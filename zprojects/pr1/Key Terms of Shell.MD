1. Oil and Gas Reserves: Reserve refers to the estimated amount of oil and gas that is known to exist in a particular area or region and is recoverable under existing technological and economic conditions.

2. Upstream: Upstream activities refer to the exploration, development, and production of oil and gas resources.

3. Downstream: Downstream activities include refining and marketing of oil and gas products, such as gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, and lubricants.

4. LNG: LNG stands for liquefied natural gas, which is natural gas that has been cooled to -162°C to convert it into a liquid form for easy transportation.

5. Exploration: Exploration activities refer to the search for new oil and gas reserves.

6. Production: Production refers to the process of extracting oil and gas from underground reservoirs.

7. Refining: Refining is the process of converting crude oil into refined products such as gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, and lubricants.

8. Petrochemicals: Petrochemicals are chemical products derived from petroleum, such as plastics, fertilizers, and solvents.

9. Renewable Energy: Renewable energy refers to energy sources that come from renewable resources that are replenished naturally, such as wind, solar, and hydropower.

10. Carbon Capture and Storage: Carbon capture and storage (CCS) is a process that captures carbon dioxide from industrial processes and stores it underground to prevent it from entering the atmosphere and contributing to climate change.
